About the Schnorr Scheme


Explanation

One of the main problems within Bitcoin that slowed down the system was how cumbersome it was to verify the signatures of different parties that partake in a transaction. The verification time drastically increases as the number of parties in the signing component increases, since each individual signature needs to be verified in isolation.

Schnorr Signature Scheme provides an efficient way to handle multi-party signatures efficiently over the elliptic curve. It takes a composite key of all of the signing parties involved, and generates a signature that can be verified with a composite public key.

Schnorr Signature Scheme Properties:

  • Each party has individual signatures generated by the different private keys not known to other parties that are also signing the key
  • All parties that are signing must agree to make the signature before it will be accepted by a verifier.
  • Key aggregation is supported. A verifier may know either the complete list of cosigner or the aggregate group key.
