About Pretty Good Primitives

This is a project for the Cryptography course at NYU Tandon School of Engineering.

Made by Eric Lin, Charlie Thomas and Kyle Timmermans.

Our project will focus on the one-way functions that we have been presented in class. Our questionaire asks users to weight between speed, collision resistance, and reversal hardness. We cover the following functions taught in class:

  • Factoring
  • Discrete Log
  • Elliptic Curve
  • Cryptography Hash
  • Post-quantum Functions

We hope that this would help you make an informed decision in your application!
