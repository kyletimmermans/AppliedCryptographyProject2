About Lamport Signature Scheme

In a world where general Quantum Computing machines is a reality...

If you run an organization that must mitigate against such dangers, then take a look at the Lamport Signature Scheme. This scheme uses modern day cryptography standards and assumes the dangers of quantum computing, yet produces a signature that is resistant to the attacks that quantum computers may bring.

The main disadvantage of a Lamport signature is that for every single message, you must generate new public keys as well as private keys. This will become very computationally expensive with an increasing amount of messages, but that is unfortunately the price to pay in order to be quantum resistant.

The gist of the scheme is that Alice creates a key-pair (public and private key). The private key consists of two sets of 256 random 256-bit numbers. This is the private key of 512 values. Next, we take the hash of each of the random numbers that was generated in the private key, this will give 512 hashes and will be the public key.
