About Gottesman-Chuang Signature Scheme

Ever since the inception of Quantum Computing and Shor's Algorithm...

Quantum Speedup attacks that breaks existing algorithms that are widely used for cryptography is a very real threat in the future. In 2001, Daniel Gottessman and Isaac Chuang published a paper detailing a new way of answering this looming threat. They published their findins in a paper called Quantum Digital Signatures.

There are no commercial implementation of this algorithm out in the wild. Currently, Quantum Digital Signatures have only been theorized and tested in laboratory settings. One of the recent implementations of Quantum Digital Signature was published in a Nature Communications paper published in November 2012. The paper details a way of representing qubits using phase-encoded states of light, and the qubit is transferred to other parties using optical fibre and photons.

This does achieve sending a message that cannot be forged or repudiated based on the limits of information theory and quantum mechanics. You can read more about this implementation below.
